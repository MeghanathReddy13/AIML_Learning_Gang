## The Most Important Document

**`WHY_STATISTICS_IS_NECESSARY.md`**

Read this first. Understand it completely. Then everything else makes sense.

Key ideas:
1. Rules assume certainty. The world is uncertain.
2. Rules fail because variation is irreducible.
3. Hidden factors exist. You can't measure everything.
4. Statistics doesn't fight uncertainty. It quantifies it.
5. The three pillars emerge naturally from these insights.

If you skip this document, students will ask "Why do we need statistics?" and you won't have a good answer.

---

## Teaching Implication

When you teach Week 1, start with WHY_STATISTICS_IS_NECESSARY.md content:

**Day 1 Message**:
"Rules fail because the world has variation. Hidden factors exist. Exceptions are infinite. Statistics handles this by learning from distributions instead of writing rules."

**Then everything else clicks**:
- Week 2: "How do we see patterns in distributions?"
- Week 3: "What statistics describe distributions?"
- Week 4: "What shapes do distributions take?"
- Week 5: "How confident are we?"
- Week 6: "How do we predict?"
